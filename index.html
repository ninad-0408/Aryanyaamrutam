Habitats in Indian Wildlife Sancturies...

Wetlands

Keoladeo Ghana National Park, Rajasthan

This park hosts a large number of avifauna during the summers and is considered as one of the best national parks to observe exotic migratory birds and other birds of India. Located in Bharatpur district of Rajasthan, this park serves as a primary centre for ornithologists and other zoological studies.

Lush Forest

Bandipur National Park, Karnataka

The Bandipur National Park is the most popular among all the national parks in India. It is a natural home to Elephants and several other endangered species. Lush forest of the Deccan Plateau and the Western Ghats makes this park one of the beautiful parks in India.

Explore rare species

Kaziranga National Park, Assam

Kaziranga National Park is the only natural habitat of the endangered One-Horned Rhinos in India as well as in the world. Located in the Golaghat district, Kaziranga National Park is one of the largest wildlife sanctuaries to explore the wildlife of Northeast India.

Diversity

Sundarbans National Park, West Bengal

Sunderbans National Park is one of the largest and oldest in India. Located in the deltaic region of India, this park also shares its boundaries with Bangladesh. Covered with mangrove forests, Sundarbans is the original abode of the stunning Royal Bengal Tigers.
